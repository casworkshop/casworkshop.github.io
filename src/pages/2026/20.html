Mental Health Risk Assessment Using Natural Language Processing on Social Media Posts

Sai Phanindra Kandula1, Madhusudan Reddy D2, Gayathri Sai Priya Ch1, Lakshmi Sai Srimukhi V1
1BV RAJU INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY, 2B V RAJU INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY

Abstract

Mental health concerns such as depression, anxiety, and suicidal ideation are increasingly expressed on social media long before individuals seek professional help. These online expressions often contain subtle emotional and linguistic cues that can serve as early indicators of psychological distress. This project aims to develop an intelligent, automated system capable of assessing mental-health risk from publicly available social media posts using Natural Language Processing (NLP) and machine learning techniques . The approach involves collecting anonymized social-media text, preprocessing it to remove noise, and extracting meaningful linguistic patterns through transformer-based models such as BERT. The system classifies posts into risk levels—no risk, low, moderate, or high—based on emotional tone, sentiment shifts, and context-aware features. By integrating explainability methods, the model highlights the specific words or phrases contributing to the prediction, ensuring transparency and trustworthiness . This project does not diagnose mental illness but assists in early detection and awareness by identifying potential warning signs. The findings demonstrate that NLP-driven models can significantly support mental-health monitoring, providing a foundation for future tools that enable timely intervention and promote emotional well-being.
